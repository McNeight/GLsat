Drawing Points

Ideal

A point is an OpenGL geometric primitive and is represented by a single vertex. The vertex itself is specified by a tuple of floating point numbers. By default, a point is drawn as a single pixel on the screen at the coordinates specified by the vertex. The vertex itself is specified by the glVertex2f(x, y) or glVertex3f(x, y, z) commands. Following a glBegin(GL_POINTS) command we declare the successive vertices. As many points are drawn as there are vertices. Thus glBegin(GL_POINTS) can preface the declaration of a sequence of points, not just a single point. To control the size of a point, we use the glPointSize(GLFfloat size) when a point is drawn as a size x size square of pixels..
