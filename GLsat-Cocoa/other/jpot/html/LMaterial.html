Material Property

 Idea

The perceived color of a material depends not only on the light source but as well, of course, on the material's own reflectance properties. As for a light source, a material's reflectance properties (ambient, diffuse and specular) may vary independently for each of the three primary colors red, green and blue. In addition, a material's appearance is determined by its shininess (which determines its reflection of specular light) as well as its emmisiveness (which is when it acts as a light source itself).

The following example allows you to set the ambient, diffuse, and specular intensities for each of the red, green, and blue reflectance components of the material. You can also change shininess and emission.
