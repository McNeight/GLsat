Perspective Projection

Idea

gluPerspective() is a library routine built on top of glFrustum() that is easier to use. This routine creates a viewing volume of the same shape as glFrustum() does, but we specify it in a different way: Rather than specifying corners of the near clipping plane, we specify the angle of the field of view in the y direction and the aspect ratio of the width to height (x/y). These are enough to determine an untruncated pyramid along the line of sight. We also specify the distance between the viewpoint and the near and far clipping planes to truncate the pyramid. Here is the form of the routine

void gluPerspective( fovy, aspect, near, far )

where fovy is the angle at the apex along y-z plane and aspect is the width / height of the rectangular top face of the frustum. near and far are as the glFrustum(), which give the distance from the viewpoint to the near and far clipping plane. Both near and far should be positive.
